Why This Yoga Programme Is Different

01

Inside a Hospital, Not a Studio

Every programme runs within Saravanan Hospital — coordinated with your treating doctor, not separate from them. Yoga here is part of your care plan.

02

Instructors Who Know Your Condition

Our yoga instructors are trained in therapeutic applications — they understand medical conditions, physical limitations, and how to adapt sessions accordingly.

03

No Experience Needed

These programmes are built for patients — not yoga enthusiasts. If you've never done yoga before, that's completely fine. Sessions start where you are.

04

Complements Every Other Treatment

Whether you're seeing our cardiologist, obstetrician, or general physician — yoga here works alongside your existing treatment, not in isolation.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Do I need prior yoga experience to join? +

No. Every programme is designed for complete beginners. You'll never be asked to do something your body isn't ready for.

Can I join if I have a medical condition like diabetes or a heart problem? +

Yes — that's exactly who these programmes are designed for. Inform your instructor of your condition before the first session so the programme is adapted appropriately.

Is prenatal yoga safe in the first trimester? +

With appropriate modifications, yes. We recommend getting clearance from your obstetrician first. Women in high-risk pregnancies should consult their doctor before joining.

How often should I attend sessions to see a benefit? +

Two to three sessions per week produces noticeable benefit within 4–6 weeks for most participants. Consistency matters more than frequency.

Can yoga replace my medication for conditions like diabetes or hypertension? +

No — and we will never suggest it does. Yoga supports medical treatment. Medication decisions are made by your doctor based on your clinical results.
